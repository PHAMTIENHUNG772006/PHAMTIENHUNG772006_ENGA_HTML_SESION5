Taylor Swift

Taylor Alison Swift (born December 13, 1989) is an American singer-songwriter. Known for her autobiographical songwriting and the subject of widespread media coverage, with a vast fanbase known as Swifties.

Swift signed to Big Machine Records in 2005, debuting as a country singer with the albums Taylor Swift (2006) and Fearless (2008). She found crossover success on country and pop radio formats. She incorporated rock on Speak Now (2010) and electronic pop on 1989 (2014); the ensuing media scrutiny inspired the hip-hop-infused Reputation (2017). The albums contained the hits "Shake It Off", "Blank Space", "Bad Blood", and "Look What You Made Me Do".
